Ongoing Planning

A relationship, not a transaction.

A financial plan is a snapshot in time - life isn't. With an ongoing retainer, your plan stays current as markets, tax rules and your circumstances change.

The decisions that matter most are made every year.

When can you draw from which account? How much, in what order, to minimize tax this year? When should you start CPP and OAS? Should you adjust after a strong - or weak - market? These aren't one-time answers. They change annually, and small adjustments compound into hundreds of thousands of dollars over a retirement.

An ongoing retainer keeps your plan living and breathing, so you're never left guessing between meetings.

  • Annual review and refresh of your full plan
  • Proactive tax & withdrawal-sequencing adjustments
  • CPP, OAS and pension timing kept optimal
  • Guidance through life events - sale, inheritance, illness, market swings
  • Estate and beneficiary updates as your family changes
  • Your questions answered year-round - no clock running

The Case

Two years early, and more to spend.

The client thought he needed to work two more years before retiring - but with the financial plan, this couple now saw that they have more than enough money to fund their current lifestyle for the remainder of their lives. The client promptly provided his retirement notice to his boss and retired two months later - two years earlier than expected.

The cost of the financial plan for these clients was $4,500 - well worth it, as the financial benefits of the plan far outweigh the cost of the plan.

  • Lifetime tax reduced by $173,645.
  • Net lifetime financial benefit of $358,645 for this couple.
  • They wanted to take OAS at 65, but it would be clawed back immediately. By restructuring how they draw their RRSPs and starting OAS at 68, we deferred any OAS clawback until age 91 - and then only minimal clawback in the last few years.
  • By determining the best time to begin CPP & OAS, they will receive an additional $60,000 and $125,000 respectively by age 95.
  • By reallocating their retirement savings/income withdrawals, they can increase their spending in the first five years of retirement by an additional $60,000 per year for travel.
