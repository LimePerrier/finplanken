# Professional Referral One-Pager

## A fee-only planning partner for incorporated professionals, business owners, and families with complexity

Clients with corporations and complex family wealth often ask planning questions that go beyond one tax return:

- Should I pay myself salary, dividends, or a mix?
- How do I get money out of my corporation tax-efficiently?
- Can my corporate and personal assets support retirement?
- What happens to retained earnings when I retire, sell, or wind down?
- How do I coordinate RRSP/RRIF, TFSA, non-registered, and corporate assets?
- How do I pass wealth to my children fairly?
- What happens to my plan if markets, health, family, or tax rules change?

Bracket Planning provides independent, fee-only, advice-only financial planning for clients whose personal, corporate, tax, retirement, and estate decisions need to work together.

We do not sell investment products. We do not charge a percentage of assets. We do not replace the client's accountant. We help clients make better long-term decisions, then coordinate with their existing professional team where appropriate.

## Why professionals refer clients to Bracket Planning

### No product sales

Bracket Planning is fee-only and advice-only. Clients receive objective planning advice without pressure to move assets, buy products, or enter an AUM relationship.

### Compensation and corporate cash-flow planning

Planning can include salary, dividends, retained earnings, corporate investments, personal spending needs, and long-term withdrawal strategy.

### Tax-aware retirement income

Many retirement decisions are tax decisions made over time: RRSP/RRIF withdrawals, taxable income, CPP/OAS timing, pension income, OAS clawbacks, corporate withdrawals, and estate tax exposure.

### Estate and succession issues

Ken's work often includes estate equalization, intergenerational wealth transfer, trusts, capital gains funding, family business or farm succession, and preserving family harmony.

### Clear process and transparent fees

Fees are disclosed, discussed, and agreed upon up front before the engagement begins.
