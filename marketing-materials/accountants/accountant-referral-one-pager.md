# Accountant Referral One-Pager

## A fee-only planning partner for the client questions that go beyond the tax return

Your clients already ask big financial questions during tax season:

- Can I afford to retire?
- Which accounts should I draw from first?
- Should I take CPP and OAS now or later?
- How do I get money out of my corporation tax-efficiently?
- Will OAS clawbacks become a problem?
- How do I pass wealth to my children fairly?
- What happens to my plan if markets, health, family, or tax rules change?

Bracket Planning provides independent, fee-only, advice-only financial planning for clients who need a coordinated roadmap across retirement income, tax, investments, insurance, estate, corporate planning, and family wealth transfer.

We do not sell investment products. We do not charge a percentage of assets. We do not replace the client's accountant. We help clients make better long-term decisions, then coordinate with their existing professional team where appropriate.

## Why accountants refer clients to Ken

### No product sales

Ken is fee-only and advice-only. Clients receive objective planning advice without pressure to move assets, buy products, or enter an AUM relationship.

### Tax-aware retirement planning

Many retirement decisions are tax decisions made over time: RRSP/RRIF withdrawals, taxable income, CPP/OAS timing, pension income, OAS clawbacks, corporate withdrawals, and estate tax exposure.

### Strong fit for incorporated professionals

Ken works with professionals and business owners who need planning across salary, dividends, retained earnings, corporate investments, personal retirement goals, and succession.

### Helpful for high-net-worth families

Ken's work often includes estate equalization, intergenerational wealth transfer, trusts, capital gains funding, family business or farm succession, and preserving family harmony.

### Clear process and transparent fees

Fees are disclosed, discussed, and agreed upon up front before the engagement begins.

## Good clients to refer

- Near-retirees who need a tax-efficient retirement income plan
- Recently retired clients unsure how to draw from investments
- Incorporated professionals with retained earnings or corporate investments
- Business owners considering succession, wind-down, or sale
- High-net-worth families with estate or equalization concerns
