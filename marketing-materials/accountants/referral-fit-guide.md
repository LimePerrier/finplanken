# Referral Fit Guide

## Who is a good fit for Bracket Planning?

Bracket Planning provides independent, fee-only, advice-only financial planning for incorporated professionals, business owners, and families with complexity. The best fit is a client whose personal, corporate, tax, and estate decisions need to work together.

The team is especially well suited for clients who need coordinated planning across compensation, retained earnings, retirement income, tax efficiency, estate planning, insurance review, and family wealth transfer.

## Ideal referral profiles

### Incorporated professionals

Doctors, dentists, lawyers, consultants, and other incorporated professionals who need planning across salary, dividends, retained earnings, corporate investments, personal retirement income, and long-term tax strategy.

### Business owners

Clients planning for succession, corporate wind-down, retained earnings withdrawal, shareholder agreements, key person planning, or transition from business income to retirement income.

### Families with complexity

Families with estate equalization concerns, trusts, gifting decisions, intergenerational wealth transfer, capital gains exposure, blended family issues, or a desire to preserve family harmony.

### Retiring professionals and owners

Clients who need to coordinate corporate assets, RRSP/RRIF withdrawals, CPP/OAS timing, taxable income, and sustainable spending.

### Clients with retained earnings

Clients who need to clarify what stays corporate, what moves personal, and how withdrawals affect tax, retirement income, and estate value.

## Common situations to refer

- A client asks how to pay themselves from a corporation.
- A client is unsure how to draw from RRSPs, RRIFs, TFSAs, non-registered accounts, or corporate assets.
- A client wants help timing CPP or OAS.
- A client is concerned about OAS clawbacks.
- A client has retained earnings inside a corporation and no clear withdrawal plan.
- A client needs to coordinate personal and corporate retirement income.
- A client has a large unrealized capital gain.
- A client needs estate equalization among children.
- A client is selling a business or major asset.
- A client has investments with no clear strategy.
- A client wants advice but does not want to be sold financial products.

## Who may not be the right fit

- Clients looking only for investment management
- Clients who want free advice before deciding whether to engage
- Clients with very simple finances and no current planning need
- Clients who primarily need tax return preparation
- Clients who primarily need legal drafting, such as wills or trusts
- Clients seeking a product recommendation without a broader plan

Bracket Planning can coordinate with accountants, lawyers, and investment professionals, but does not replace their roles.

## How the professional relationship works

The goal is to support the client while respecting the accountant's existing relationship.

Bracket Planning helps the client clarify the planning strategy, model decisions, and identify tax-sensitive tradeoffs. The accountant remains the client's tax preparation and tax compliance professional.

Where appropriate, Ken can coordinate with the accountant around:

- Salary and dividend planning
- Retained earnings strategy
- RRSP/RRIF withdrawal strategy
- CPP and OAS timing
- Corporate withdrawals
- Taxable income smoothing
- Capital gains planning
- Estate and beneficiary considerations
- Business succession or wind-down planning

## How clients work with Ken

### Step 1: Introductory call

The client discusses the situation, goals, complexity, and whether planning support is a fit.

### Step 2: Information gathering

The client provides the details needed to build a comprehensive picture: income, assets, liabilities, investments, pensions, insurance, tax details, corporate information, family context, and estate goals.

### Step 3: Comprehensive financial plan

Bracket Planning builds a roadmap covering the relevant areas of the client's financial life, such as compensation, corporate assets, retirement income, tax, investments, risk, estate, and wealth transfer.

### Step 4: Recommendations and implementation priorities

The client receives clear, plain-language recommendations and a prioritized action list.

### Step 5: Ongoing planning, when appropriate

For clients with evolving decisions, Bracket Planning can provide ongoing retainer support, annual reviews, and year-round access for questions.
